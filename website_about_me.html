Background

Growing up in New Boston, NH, I had over 300 acres of land in my backyard. I spent my time hiking, skiing, mountain biking, fishing, playing sports, and generally rooting around the woods, needless to say my love for the outdoors has been a constant my entire life. From the ages of 10-19 (or something like that), I didn't spend nearly as much time ourdoors. I focused on team sports, like volleyball and soccer, giving up a lot of skiing, camping, and hiking. Around 2018, I moved to Vermont and fell back into the world of skiing and hiking. Ever since then skiing has had a grip on me, and I don't see that stopping any time soon.

While I didn't start taking photography seriously until 2020, I was always the first one to take a video of friends skiing a cool line, or a photo of them on top of a mountain. I always knew I liked creating content, I just never took the first step. Now that I take it more seriously, I love seeing my own growth, and try to regularly draw inspiration from other photographers.

Education

I graduated from Goffstown High School in 2017 and headed straight to college. After moving between a few schools, I earned my Associates Degree in Business Studies, and enrolled at the University of New Hampshire, where I am currently working towards my Bachelors Degree in Communication Arts: Digital Media Studies. As I mentioned before, in 2018 I moved to Vermont, where I took classes at Saint Michael's College and learned how amazing Vermont is.

The parallell timeline of me revisiting the outdoor activities I love, and getting into photography have absolutely benefited each other. I find that being motivated to do one half motivates me to do the other in turn, so once I began making both a bigger part of my life, I was sold, realizing that this is what I wanted to do with my life if possible. I'm excited to see what is in the cards for me after college!

My Style

Like most people, I photograph the things I enjoy doing. For me this means skiing, hiking, and landscape photography, but I've branched out and have photographed volleyball, portraits, buildings, and wedding invites as well. When I shoot, I try to keep things as natural as possible. I want to create photographs that help people place themselves into the scene rather than create a photograph that feels artificial, or almost fake. I do want to explore that side of photography, because it is amazing how powerful our technology is, but I think keeping photos natural is valuable as well.
